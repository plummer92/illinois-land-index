What to check

Chatham land searches should start with parcel facts, not listing photos.

Acreage Buckets

Separate small residential lots from larger acreage, farm ground, vacant land, and edge-of-growth parcels.

Owner Patterns

Review owner rollups, related holdings, entity names, trusts, mailing addresses, and repeated ownership signals.

Tax Timing

Check annual taxes, unpaid balances, due-soon installments, and tax-search links before considering outreach.

Terrain and Access

Screen slope, low ground, road frontage, nearby development, and county GIS boundaries before a drive-by.

How to use the Chatham research page

Start in the live dashboard, filter around Chatham and nearby Sangamon County parcels, then inspect parcel detail links. The best early candidates usually combine enough acreage, a realistic owner profile, clean county records, and fewer terrain or access questions.

Why Chatham deserves its own search

Chatham has a different buyer profile than remote rural acreage. It can include growth-edge land, hobby acreage, estate holdings, farm fragments, and parcels where taxes or ownership history make the property worth a closer look.
